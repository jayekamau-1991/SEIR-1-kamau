My Study Notes

----------------------------------------
WHAT IS IT?
----------------------------------------
Identity is the system that controls WHO is trusted to do WHAT
in a cloud environment.

It is not a feature or a setting.
It is the CONTROL PLANE of a cloud system.

Two rules to memorize:
    Infrastructure decides what CAN EXIST.
    Identity decides WHO IS TRUSTED to use it.

Example: You can build the most secure server in the world.
If identity is misconfigured, the wrong person gets in —
or the right person gets locked out.
Either way, the infrastructure doesn't matter.

----------------------------------------
KEY SERVICES & TERMS
----------------------------------------
Identity
    The system that answers: "Who are you and should I trust you?"
    Controls access to people, data, and infrastructure.

Authentication
    PROVING WHO YOU ARE.
    Question it answers: "Are you who you claim to be?"
    Example: You enter your password. System confirms your identity.
    KEY POINT: Authentication alone does NOT give you access.

Authorization
    PROVING WHAT YOU ARE ALLOWED TO DO.
    Question it answers: "Are you permitted to do this action?"
    Example: You are logged in as a developer but your role
             only allows READ access, not WRITE access.
    KEY POINT: Authorization happens AFTER authentication.

Trust
    A configured relationship where one system accepts
    identity claims from another system.
    Question it answers: "Do I accept what this other system tells me?"
    Example: GCP trusts Microsoft Entra ID to verify your identity.
    KEY POINT: Trust is NOT automatic. It is deliberate and fragile.

Principal
    Who a policy applies to.
    Can be a user, a service account, or a group.

Least Privilege
    Only grant the MINIMUM access needed to do the job. Nothing more.

Blast Radius
    How much damage can happen if an identity change goes wrong.
    Small = one resource affected.
    Large = entire organization affected.

Lockout
    When a valid user or system LOSES ACCESS due to an identity failure.
    A lockout IS an outage. It stops work just like a server going down.

Audit Trail / Log
    The record of who did what and when.
    Mantra: "If it's not logged, it didn't happen."

----------------------------------------
HOW IT WORKS
----------------------------------------
Identity works in layers inside a cloud system:

Step 1: A user or service tries to access a resource.
Step 2: The identity provider authenticates the user.
        (Are you who you say you are?)
Step 3: The cloud platform checks authorization.
        (Are you allowed to do this action on this resource?)
Step 4: If BOTH checks pass → access granted.
Step 5: Every step is LOGGED.

CRITICAL POINT:
Authentication and authorization are SEPARATE.
Passing authentication does NOT guarantee authorization.
You can prove who you are and STILL be denied.

Trust is the glue between layers.
If trust breaks (certificate expires, config changes),
the ENTIRE chain breaks — even if credentials are valid.

----------------------------------------
THINGS TO REMEMBER
----------------------------------------
1. Identity failures cause outages.
   A server going down is visible and recoverable.
   An identity failure can SILENTLY lock out an entire team.
   Recovery is much harder.

2. Access recovery is harder than deployment rollback.
   Bad code? Roll it back.
   Bad identity config? You may have locked out the account
   needed to FIX the mistake. This is why identity changes
   must be slow, deliberate, and always have a recovery plan.

3. Authentication is NOT authorization.
   Logging in successfully ≠ you can do what you need to do.
   BOTH layers must work.

4. Trust is fragile.
   Trust relationships break due to:
   - Certificate expiration
   - Configuration changes
   - Policy drift
   When trust breaks, EVERYONE who depends on it loses access
   at the same time.

5. Blast radius always matters.
   Before ANY identity change ask:
   "If this goes wrong, what is the maximum damage?"
   Start small. Test in isolation.
   NEVER change production identity config without a recovery plan.

6. Logs are evidence.
   Gut feelings and screenshots are NOT evidence.
   Logs ARE. If you can't find it in a log, you can't prove it happened.

7. AI is not the decision maker.
   AI can help you analyze.
   AI cannot and should NOT make identity decisions.
   YOU are accountable for every change you make.

----------------------------------------
REAL WORLD ANALOGY
----------------------------------------
Think of a large HOSPITAL.

Infrastructure = the building, rooms, and equipment.
Identity       = the badge system controlling who enters which room.

Authentication = your badge identifies you as Dr. Smith.
Authorization  = your badge allows you into operating rooms
                 but NOT the pharmacy.
Trust          = the hospital trusts badges issued by its own HR system.

Lockout scenario:
Dr. Smith's badge stops working at 2am during surgery prep.
The OR still exists. The equipment still works.
But the surgeon cannot get in.
THAT IS AN OUTAGE.

Access recovery:
Getting Dr. Smith back in WITHOUT compromising the pharmacy.
This takes time, deliberate steps, and a chain of trusted people.
You cannot just override the badge system blindly.

----------------------------------------
PROGRAM MANTRAS (MEMORIZE THESE)
----------------------------------------

2. "Identity changes are slow, deliberate, and high-impact."
3. "If it's not logged, it didn't happen."
4. "Shift left — chase failure to learn."
5. "AI is a bounded assistant. Never the decision maker."
